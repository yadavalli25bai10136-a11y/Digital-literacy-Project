# Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted entity.

A common phishing scenario begins when a student receives an email or message that appears to be from a legitimate source, such as a bank or a well-known company. The message often creates urgency, such as claiming that the user’s account will be blocked unless immediate action is taken. It contains a link that redirects the user to a fake website designed to look identical to the original platform.

Once the user enters their login credentials or financial details, the attacker captures this information. In some cases, the victim may also receive a call requesting an OTP, which the attacker uses to complete unauthorized transactions.

Students and young adults are frequent targets because they are highly active online and may not always verify sources carefully. The consequences include financial loss, unauthorized access to accounts, identity theft, and emotional distress.

This case highlights the importance of verifying links, avoiding suspicious messages, and never sharing sensitive information online.
