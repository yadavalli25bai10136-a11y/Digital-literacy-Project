# Introduction

This project was undertaken as part of the Digital Literacy course to develop essential skills required for navigating the modern digital world. As a Student Digital Ambassador, the objective was to understand, apply, and promote responsible and effective use of digital platforms. The project consists of five tasks, each focusing on a different aspect of digital literacy including awareness, professional presence, platform usage, communication, and cybersecurity.

---

# Task 1: Digital Literacy Infographic

I created a digital literacy infographic using Canva, a free and user-friendly design tool. The infographic highlights key aspects such as the definition of digital literacy, essential tools for students, safe internet practices, and professional online presence. The design was structured with a clean layout, icons, and minimal text to ensure clarity and visual appeal.

One interesting aspect of this task was learning how to present information concisely while maintaining visual engagement. I found it slightly challenging to balance content and design, as including too much information made the infographic cluttered. This task helped me understand the importance of simplicity and effective communication in digital design.

# Task 2: Student Digital Portfolio

For my digital portfolio, I created and updated profiles on GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle. GitHub is used to showcase coding projects and track development progress. # Task 3: Digital Platform Survey

I created a Google Form survey to understand how students use digital platforms for learning and professional development. The survey included questions related to platform usage, awareness of digital literacy, and online behavior.

The purpose of this survey was to analyze student engagement with digital tools and identify gaps in awareness. Through this task, I learned how to design structured questions and collect meaningful data. It also helped me understand the importance of data-driven decision-making in improving digital literacy among students.

---

# Task 4: Email Etiquette and Social Media Guide

In this task, I drafted two professional emails: one requesting an assignment extension and another expressing interest in an internship opportunity. Both emails followed proper structure, tone, and etiquette guidelines.

Additionally, I created a social media Do’s and Don’ts checklist to promote responsible online behavior. This task improved my understanding of professional communication and the importance of maintaining a respectful and clear tone in digital interactions.

A situation where poor digital communication can cause problems is when a student sends an informal or unclear email to a professor, resulting in miscommunication or being ignored. This can be avoided by using proper subject lines, respectful language, and structured content.

---

# Task 5: Cybercrime Awareness

For this task, I explored phishing as a common cybercrime. Phishing involves tricking users into revealing sensitive information through fake emails or websites. I described how such attacks occur, who they target, and their consequences.

I also created a prevention checklist with actionable steps such as verifying links, avoiding sharing OTPs, and using secure platforms. Reporting channels like the National Cyber Crime Portal and helpline 1930 were included.

While researching this topic, I was surprised by how simple yet effective phishing attacks can be. I realized that awareness plays a major role in prevention. One habit I will change is carefully verifying all online communications before taking action.
